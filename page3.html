Graduate Students

Benjamin Donovan

Benjamin Donovan is a graduate student in the McEntaffer lab. He is the lead graduate student on a suborbital rocket experiment, the Off-plane Grating Rocket Experiment (OGRE). In addition, he is developing methodology to realize X-ray gratings on non-planar surfaces as a NASA Space Technology Research Fellow. He obtained undergraduate degrees in physics and astronomy from the University of Iowa. Go Hawks! In his free time, he enjoys golfing, hiking, and playing the piano.

Jake McCoy

Born in Illinois and raised in New Hampshire, he attended UMass Amherst where he briefly studied observational radio astronomy and earned a B.S. in Astronomy and Physics. During these undergraduate years, he also attended the University of Oslo for one semester. He started his graduate career at the University of Iowa where he was first introduced to X-ray instrumentation through working on lab testing of a silicon drift detector with Prof. Kaaret.
When he first joined the McEntaffer group, he worked on developing a telemetry interface using FPGAs and associated electronics for the OGRESS mission. Later, he began to study the nanofabrication of gratings at Iowa under a NASA Space Technology Research Fellowship. A year into this project, the McEntaffer group made the move to Penn State, where work continued at the Materials Research Institute. Primarily, his research in nanofab focuses on using grayscale e-beam lithography coupled with thermal reflow to make blazed gratings. He also studies nanoimprint lithography to produce grating replicas and is involved with testing gratings for diffraction efficiency at the Advanced Light Source of Lawrence-Berkeley National Lab.
